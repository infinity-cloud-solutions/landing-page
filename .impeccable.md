## Design Context

### Users
Small business owners — non-technical, busy, results-driven. They don't care how the tech works; they care whether their business will grow because of it. They're choosing between this and doing nothing, not between this and a technical competitor. The interface must communicate value in seconds, build trust fast, and make the path to action feel obvious and low-risk.

### Brand Personality
Result-focused, growth-oriented, innovation you can trust.
In concrete terms: **bold, dependable, alive.**
Not a cold AI company. Not a corporate enterprise vendor. A partner that launches small businesses into a new trajectory — hence the rocket concept as the core identity.

### Aesthetic Direction
- References: voxr.ai (benefit-first headlines, clean card grids, professional credibility), assistantly.com (approachable, playful energy with startup confidence), sowieso wero-wallet.eu (bold declarative type, scroll storytelling, institutional trust through whitespace)
- Anti-references: generic AI landing pages — neon on dark, split hero with stock photo left + text right, purple-to-blue gradient text, hollow tech buzzwords
- Theme: dark is appropriate — business owners look at growth dashboards at night, the rocket-in-space concept calls for it, and it differentiates from reference sites that all default to light
- Logo: rocket + cloud — the rocket must appear in the hero; the cloud represents the destination (the infinite possibility of the cloud / AI)
- i18n: Spanish + English, Mexico-based company

### Design Principles
1. **Clarity of outcome over cleverness of tech** — every headline must state a result, not a feature. Small business owners buy outcomes.
2. **The rocket earns its place** — it's not decoration; it's the brand story. The rocket traveling to cloudy space = the client's business being launched into a new chapter.
3. **Trust through evidence** — testimonials, metrics, and the "Made in Mexico" local-partner signal matter more than visual effects. Effects attract; evidence converts.
4. **Motion serves meaning** — the trail reveal is not just eye candy; it dramatizes the promise of "transformation." Every animation must have a reason.
5. **One wow moment, then convert** — the hero does the showstopper. The rest of the page should be clean, credible, and fast to scan — not competing with the hero for attention.

### Typography Direction
Brand words: bold, dependable, alive.
- Display: Clash Display or Bebas Neue — broad, unapologetic, aerospace-poster energy
- Body: Geist or Mona Sans — clean, modern, readable at small sizes
- Avoid: Inter, Plus Jakarta Sans (current), DM Sans, Outfit (all reflex fonts)

### Color Direction
- Base: deep space near-black (oklch ~10% L, ~0.01 C, 265°)
- Surface: rich indigo-navy (oklch ~14% L)
- Accent primary: electric gold/amber (oklch ~75% L, ~0.15 C, 60°) — warmth, growth, energy
- Accent secondary: bright teal (oklch ~70% L, ~0.15 C, 195°) — tech, sky, infinite
- Text: warm near-white (oklch ~95% L, ~0.01 C, 90°)
- Neutrals tinted toward brand indigo hue

### Technical Constraints
- Next.js 14 App Router, TypeScript, Tailwind CSS v4, GSAP 3.13 + ScrollTrigger + MotionPathPlugin, Lenis smooth scroll
- Must respect prefers-reduced-motion
- Must work in both ES and EN (i18n context already in place)
- Rocket concept must be present in hero
